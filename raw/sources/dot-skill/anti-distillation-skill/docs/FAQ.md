# Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### What is Incinerate.skill?

Incinerate.skill is a defensive tool designed to protect your professional identity from being distilled into a permanent AI skill. When employees leave companies, their knowledge, communication patterns, and decision-making processes are increasingly being captured to create "digital employees." This tool helps prevent that by strategically polluting your data trail.

### Is this legal?

This tool is designed for **defensive purposes only**. It helps you:
- Protect your personal communication style
- Prevent unauthorized use of your professional identity
- Ensure your knowledge isn't commodified without consent

It does **not**:
- Delete or destroy company data
- Leak confidential information
- Violate employment agreements

Always consult local laws and your employment contract.

### Won't this affect my work relationships?

The pollution is designed to be:
- **Subtle**: Most humans won't notice the variations
- **Explainable**: Can be attributed to "mood" or "context"
- **Professional**: Never inappropriate or harmful content

## Technical Questions

### How does distillation work?

AI distillation systems analyze your:
- Communication patterns (Slack, email, meetings)
- Documentation (wikis, knowledge bases)
- Decision history (PR reviews, meeting notes)
- Behavioral patterns (response times, preferences)

They use these to create a model that can respond "like you" to future queries.

### How does pollution defeat distillation?

Pollution breaks the assumptions distillation systems rely on:

| Assumption | How Pollution Breaks It |
|------------|------------------------|
| Consistency | Inject contradictory communication styles |
| Coherence | Fragment knowledge with contradictions |
| Predictability | Randomize behavior patterns |
| Authenticity | Embed traps and time bombs |

### What are the different pollution modes?

| Mode | Pollution % | Use Case | Detection Risk |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| `subtle` | 5-15% | Long-term defense while employed | Very Low |
| `aggressive` | 20-35% | 1-2 months before departure | Low |
| `chaos` | 40-60% | Final week before departure | Medium |

### What are traps?

Traps are specially designed content that causes distilled skills to fail:

- **Factual Traps**: Plausible but incorrect information
- **Logical Traps**: Contradictory statements spread across documents
- **Temporal Traps**: Time-sensitive information that becomes outdated
- **Trigger Traps**: Specific keywords that cause anomalous responses

## Usage Questions

### How do I know if I'm being distilled?

Look for these signals:

**Communication**:
- Frequent questions about your methodology
- Requests for "standard templates"
- Excessive screenshot-taking of your messages

**Documentation**:
- Being asked to "systematize your knowledge"
- Requests to write detailed work manuals
- Systematic collection of your documents

**Behavioral**:
- Invitations to "knowledge management projects"
- HR conducting "experience transfer" interviews
- Your work being over-documented

### When should I start using this?

Ideally, before you need it. The best defense is continuous:

- **While employed**: Use subtle mode for ongoing protection
- **Planning to leave**: Switch to aggressive mode 1-2 months before
- **Last week**: Use chaos mode for maximum protection

### What if I'm already being distilled?

1. Run the detection checklist to confirm
2. Immediately switch to chaos mode
3. Activate all traps
4. Document everything
5. Consider legal consultation

### Can this be reversed?

All pollution is logged and explainable. You can generate an explanation document that attributes variations to:
- "Different contexts require different communication styles"
- "My views evolved over time"
- "That was my understanding at the time"

## Ethical Questions

### Is this deception?

No. This is **defensive** protection of your personal data and professional identity. Just as you might use a VPN to protect your browsing data, this protects your professional data from being used to create a permanent digital copy of you without your consent.

### What about knowledge transfer?

This tool doesn't prevent legitimate knowledge transfer. You can still:
- Document processes for your team
- Train replacements
- Complete normal handoff procedures

The pollution only affects AI distillation, not human understanding.

### Should I tell my employer?

This is a personal decision. The pollution is designed to be undetectable to humans, so disclosure is optional. However, if you're concerned about legal issues, consult with a lawyer familiar with employment and IP law in your jurisdiction.

## Troubleshooting

### The pollution seems too obvious

If pollution is noticeable:
1. Reduce pollution ratio
2. Switch to subtle mode
3. Focus on less visible data sources
4. Ensure contradictions are contextually plausible

### I'm worried about getting caught

The pollution is designed with deniability:
- All variations can be explained as normal professional behavior
- No content is inappropriate or harmful
- The goal is AI confusion, not human deception

### The tools aren't working

1. Check Python version (3.9+ required)
2. Install dependencies: `pip3 install -r requirements.txt`
3. Verify file permissions
4. Check that you're running from the correct directory
